🔴 THE 5 STEPS (SIMPLIFIED):
================================================================================

STEP 1: Collect Data
  INPUT:  Temperature, Rain, Soil Moisture, Vegetation, Urban Areas
  FROM:   Climate sensors (daily) + Satellite images (monthly)
  RESULT: 1000s of observations over 10 years
  ✅ Makes sense: These are the factors that matter for insurance

STEP 2: Train AI Models
  SHOW:   "When it was 24°C yesterday, today it's 25°C"
  REPEAT: With 1000s of examples
  LEARN:  "Temperature usually increases 0.3°C per day"
  TEST:   Does it work on unseen data? 89% accuracy ✅
  ✅ Makes sense: AI learns patterns from examples

STEP 3: Predict Future
  USE:    "Temperature has been warming 0.1°C per year"
  APPLY:  Trend for 5 years = +0.5°C
  ASSUME: Trends continue forever
  ❌ Problem: What if something changes? (Trend might break)

STEP 4: Convert to Risk Scores
  DEFINE: 15-25°C = safe (0/100 risk)
  DEFINE: 0.127mm rain = DROUGHT (99/100 risk)
  SCORE:  Each metric gets 0-100
  ❌ Problem: What if location is a desert? (Low rain is normal!)

STEP 5: Calculate Total Risk
  COMBINE: 6 metrics with weights (20%, 20%, 20%, 20%, 10%, 10%)
  FORMULA: 0.2×T + 0.2×R + 0.2×V + 0.2×M + 0.1×S + 0.1×U = RISK
  RESULT:  50.8/100 (moderate risk)
  ⚠️ Problem: Weights are generic (might be wrong for this location)

🎯 FUTURE PREDICTIONS (5 Years): MOSTLY UNRELIABLE
================================================================================

Method Used: Trend Extrapolation
  ├─ Look at how temperature changed over 10 years: +0.1°C/year
  ├─ Assume same rate continues: 5 years × 0.1°C = +0.5°C
  └─ Say: "In 5 years, temperature will be 24.73°C"

Problems:
  ├─ ❌ Climate can change rapidly (tipping points)
  ├─ ❌ Trends can reverse (El Niño, volcanic eruptions)
  ├─ ❌ Assumes future = past (dangerous assumption!)
  ├─ ❌ Precipitation trend is +0.09 mm/day over 5 years
  │     But precipitation MAPE=64%, so this is meaningless
  └─ ❌ We're predicting 5 years, not 50 (too short)

Predictions for 5 Years:
